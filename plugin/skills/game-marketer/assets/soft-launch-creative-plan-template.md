## Creative concepts (4–6 distinct angles)

| Concept | Angle | Hero verb | Tone | Hypothesis |

## Variants per concept (3 per concept)

For each concept, produce 3 variants differing in:
- Hook (different opening visuals / lines)
- Length (15s / 30s)
- Aspect (16:9 / 9:16)

Total creatives: 6 concepts × 3 variants = 18 creatives at launch.

## Refresh cadence

Creatives decay. A given creative typically peaks within 1–2 weeks then degrades. Plan creative refresh:

- **Weekly:** kill bottom-decile creatives; add 2–3 new variants
- **Bi-weekly:** introduce a new concept (rotation across the 6 angles)
- **Monthly:** comprehensive review — which concepts / hooks are winning; refresh winners

## KPIs per creative

- **CPI** (cost per install) — primary
- **CTR** (click-through rate) — leading indicator
- **CR** (conversion rate, click → install) — efficiency
- **D1 / D7 retention** of installs from this creative
- **D7 ROAS** of installs from this creative
- **D30 LTV** of installs from this creative (lagging — but the truest signal)

A creative with low CPI but bad downstream retention is *worse* than a creative with higher CPI and strong retention. Optimize for ROAS, not CPI alone.

## Playbook outputs

After soft launch:
- Top 3 concepts by ROAS — carry into global launch
- Top 3 creatives by ROAS — scale spend
- Worst-performing concepts — kill or rework
- Network mix — which networks delivered the best ROAS
- Hook patterns — common patterns across winning creatives (for next creative briefs)
- Audience signals — what targeting (geo, demo, interests) correlated with winners

## Hand-off

- **`game-monetization-strategist`:** updated CPI / ROAS data feeds the global-launch UA plan and the segment-economics model
