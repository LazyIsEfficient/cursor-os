# Monetization models

Each model has a sweet spot of design + audience + platform. Picking the wrong one is the most expensive mistake in the monetization stack — it's invisible during development and visible at launch when retention craters.

## 1. Premium (one-time purchase)

**What it is:** player pays once, plays forever. Optional DLC for additional content.

**Fits:** finite, replayable, high-craft single-player; PC / Console; story-led indies; cult brands.

**Fights:** live-ops, infinite-content, F2P-shaped concepts; mobile (premium mobile is brutal); audiences without disposable income.

**KPI shape:** units sold × price = revenue. Long tail; sales drive most LT revenue; community / reviews are load-bearing.

**Risks:** discount erosion (game-on-sale-forever), refund-rate (Steam 2-hour rule), no live revenue stream when content droughts hit.

**Comp wins:** *Hades*, *Hollow Knight*, *Disco Elysium*, *Stardew Valley* (premium with optional content).

## 2. F2P with cosmetic-only IAP

**What it is:** game is free; players buy cosmetics; cosmetics never affect gameplay power.

**Fits:** PvP, multiplayer, social, identity-strong games (cosmetics are status); Mobile / PC / Console; concepts where *other players see the cosmetics*.

**Fights:** single-player (no audience for cosmetics); games with weak identity / character investment; shallow cosmetic catalogs.

**KPI shape:** D1/D7/D30 retention drives the population; ARPU/payer is high (cosmetics are luxury); whale-skewed.

**Risks:** cosmetic catalog drought; visual clutter from too many cosmetics; "pay to be seen" backlash if cosmetics feel mandatory for status.

**Comp wins:** *Fortnite*, *Apex Legends*, *Valorant*, *League of Legends*.

## 3. F2P with progression-affecting IAP

**What it is:** game is free; players can buy advantages — currency, gear, characters, time skips.

**Fits:** mobile RPGs, gachas, idle games, builders, mid-core games; audiences accustomed to F2P spend; long-content games.

**Fights:** competitive PvP without skill differentiation (pay-to-win backlash); audiences that resent mechanical paywalls; jurisdictions with strict gambling rules.

**KPI shape:** ARPDAU is the central metric; whale concentration is high (top 1% often = 30–50% of revenue); LTV depends on D30+.

**Risks:** pay-to-win perception, regulatory pressure on loot boxes / gacha, app store scrutiny.

**Comp wins:** *Genshin Impact*, *Clash of Clans*, *Raid: Shadow Legends*, *Marvel Snap* (hybrid).

## 4. Subscription

**What it is:** recurring fee for access, ad-removal, perks, or full-content unlock.

**Fits:** service-shaped games (MMOs), GaaS with continuous content, premium-feel mobile games (Apple Arcade), ad-removal tier on top of F2P.

**Fights:** finite / completable games; concepts without a continuous content reason to stay; audiences without subscription appetite (most non-tier-1 markets).

**KPI shape:** MRR is the metric; churn rate is everything; LTV = ARPU / churn.

**Risks:** content cadence collapse → churn spike; subscription fatigue; perception that the sub buys "less" over time.

**Comp wins:** *World of Warcraft*, *Final Fantasy XIV*, *Apple Arcade* portfolio, *Xbox Game Pass*.

## 5. Ad-supported

**What it is:** game is free; ads are the primary revenue source. Often combined with rewarded-video integration.

**Fits:** casual / hyper-casual mobile, idle games, puzzle, lifestyle apps, audiences with low spend appetite.

**Fights:** premium-feel games; concepts where flow-state is the value prop; PC / console / web (ad networks are weaker outside mobile).

**KPI shape:** eCPM × ad views per DAU = ARPDAU; ad fill rate matters; user concentration matters less than reach.

**Risks:** adblock; ad-network policy changes; eCPM volatility (esp. post-IDFA changes); ad fatigue.

**Comp wins:** *Subway Surfers*, *Stack*, *Ball Sort Puzzle*, hyper-casual catalog.

## 6. Battle pass / season pass

**What it is:** time-bound progression track with free and paid tiers. Typically a 4–12 week season.

**Fits:** GaaS, F2P, live-ops games with content cadence; player base that returns daily/weekly.

**Fights:** games without content cadence; players who can't commit to a season-long engagement; finite games.

**KPI shape:** pass conversion rate (% of MAU that buy); pass completion rate (% of pass buyers that finish); per-season ARPU.

**Risks:** pass fatigue (back-to-back passes burn players out); pass length wrong (too short = anxiety; too long = churn); rewards perceived as weak.

**Comp wins:** *Fortnite*, *Apex Legends*, *Call of Duty: Mobile*, *Halo Infinite*.

## 7. Hybrid

**What it is:** any combination of the above. Most live games end up here.

**Fits:** mature studios with capacity to manage multiple revenue streams; complex audience compositions.

**Fights:** small teams (operational complexity); concepts with one clear monetization fit (hybrid would dilute).

**KPI shape:** depends on the mix; usually one model dominates and others contribute marginally.

**Risks:** mismatched messaging ("is this premium or F2P?"); operational overhead; over-monetization perception.

**Comp wins:** *Marvel Snap* (F2P + battle pass + cosmetics + bundles), *Fortnite* (cosmetic IAP + battle pass + crew sub).

## 8. Web3-native (token economy)

**What it is:** primary economy is on-chain; players earn / hold / trade tokens; tokens drive engagement and / or have real-money value.

**Fits:** concepts where trading / market-making *is* the verb; audiences with wallets and tolerance for volatility; chains with low transaction cost.

**Fights:** mass-market audiences; jurisdictions with crypto restrictions; platforms that disallow crypto IAP (App Store, Google Play, Steam); concepts where token-as-money breaks the fantasy.

**KPI shape:** token velocity / market cap, secondary market liquidity, on-chain DAU. Traditional retention/ARPDAU still apply.

**Risks:** token price collapse (sources > sinks); regulatory action; user uncomfortable with money-shaped UX; secondary market manipulation; speculation pulls focus from gameplay.

**Comp lessons:** *Axie Infinity* (boom and crash on token-source dominance), early *Splinterlands*, *Star Atlas* (presale-heavy economy).

## 9. Web3 NFT-as-content

**What it is:** in-game items / characters / land / cosmetics are NFTs; players own and trade them; secondary markets generate fees.

**Fits:** collector-as-fantasy; identity-through-items; persistent world stakes; community-curated content.

**Fights:** games requiring frequent rebalances of items; concepts where item iteration is gameplay-critical; audiences without wallets.

**KPI shape:** primary mint revenue + secondary market fees; floor price stability; collection completion metrics.

**Risks:** NFT'd content is hard to nerf without owner revolt; floor-price volatility; secondary market dries up; regulatory reclassification.

**Comp lessons:** *Bored Ape* (peripheral game value), *Gods Unchained*, *Illuvium*; many failures from over-emphasizing NFTs over gameplay.

## 10. Hybrid web2 + web3

**What it is:** game ships a web2 storefront + a web3 layer (optional wallet, optional tokens / NFTs).

**Fits:** mass-market reach + crypto-curious power users; bridging web2 audiences into web3.

**Fights:** small teams (running two storefronts is operationally heavy); concepts where the dual model muddies positioning.

**KPI shape:** majority of revenue from web2 storefronts (App Store / Google Play / Steam) + minority from web3 (mints, fees); careful comparison of segments.

**Risks:** App Store / Google Play hostility to crypto-adjacent functionality; segments cannibalize each other; messaging confusion.

**Comp lessons:** several recent attempts; outcomes mixed.

## Picking the model

Five questions:

1. **What's the player's relationship with money in the fantasy?** Money invisible → premium / ads. Money present → IAP / sub. Money central → web3.
2. **What's the session cadence?** 3-min / 4× day → ad-friendly. 30-min / 3× week → IAP / sub. 20-hour / one-and-done → premium.
3. **Where does the player live?** Mobile-only → IAP / ads. PC → premium / hybrid. Web → ads / Stripe-backed sub. Console → premium + DLC.
4. **What's the team's capacity for live ops?** None → premium. Some → F2P with battle pass. High → full GaaS hybrid.
5. **What's the content cadence the team can sustain?** Sustained content → service models work. Single drop → premium.

Match the model to the answers. Mismatches surface as KPI failures in soft launch — address them in strategy, not in tactics.
